Classic Spaghetti Carbonara

This authentic Italian dish combines creamy eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, and crispy pancetta for a rich, comforting pasta meal. Perfect for a quick yet indulgent dinner!

Recipe Information

Prep time: 15 minutes

Cook time: 10 minutes

Servings: 4

Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 12 oz spaghetti
  • 4 oz pancetta, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti until al dente, about 8-10 minutes. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water, then drain.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together eggs and Pecorino Romano cheese until smooth. Set aside.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add garlic cloves and cook for 1 minute to infuse flavor, then remove garlic.
  4. Add diced pancetta to the skillet and cook until crispy, about 5 minutes.
  5. Toss drained spaghetti into the skillet with pancetta. Remove from heat and quickly pour in the egg-cheese mixture, stirring to create a creamy sauce. Add reserved pasta water as needed to adjust consistency.
  6. Season with freshly ground black pepper and serve immediately with extra Pecorino Romano on top.

Tips

For best results, use room-temperature eggs to prevent scrambling when mixing with hot pasta. Avoid adding cream, as traditional carbonara relies on eggs for its creamy texture.
